Introduction

The diploma in Electrical and Electronics Engineering course covers a wide range of electrical and electronics engineering basic knowledge. It is to ensure that graduates have mastered the necessary skills required, knowledge and professionalism to meet the challenges in the broader electrical and electronic engineering industry. The main objective is to produce technically trained personnel who understand the electronic aspects of communication, computer, computer network, and automated systems. These technicians will play a key role in many aspects of todayâ€™s highly technical environment.

Programme Educational Objectives (POs)

The objectives of the programme are:

PEO 1

Graduates who are capable in handling engineering issues.

PEO2

Professional graduates who continue upgrading their engineering knowledge.

PEO3

Graduates that execute professional characteristics in terms of engineering practice and ethical conduct, sensitivity in sustainability issues and multi- cultural society.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

Our programme learning outcomes are listed as follows:

i. Ability to apply acquired knowledge of applied mathematics, applied science and electronic engineering to well practical procedures and process.

ii. Ability to identify and analyze well-define engineering problems.

iii. Design solution for well-define engineering problems and assist with the design of the systems, components or processes to meet specified needs with appropriate consideration for public health and safety, cultural, multi-cultural society and the environment.

iv. Ability to conduct investigation of well-define engineering problems; locate and search relevant codes and catalogues, conduct standard tests and measurements.

v. Ability to apply appropriate techniques, resources, modern engineering and IT tools to well-define engineering problems, with an awareness of limitations.

vi.Ability to demonstrate knowledge of the societal, health, safety, legal and cultural issues and the consequent responsibilities relevant to engineering technician practice and solutions to well-define engineering problems.

vii. Ability to understand and commit to professional ethics and responsibilities and norms of technician practice.

viii. Ability to understand and evaluate the sustainability and impact of engineering technician work in the solution of well-define engineering problems in societal and environment contexts.

ix. Ability to function effectively as an individual, and as a member in diverse technical teams with multi-cultural background.

x. Ability to communicate effectively on well-define engineering activities in both written and verbally in the engineering community and with multi-cultural society.

xi. Ability to recognize the needs for, and have the ability to engage in independent updating in the context of specialized technical knowledge.

xii. Ability to demonstrate knowledge and understanding of engineering management principles to manage projects as a member and a leader team, in multi-disciplinary environment.

Entry Requirements

SPM: at least 3 credits include Mathematics and Science related subject and a pass in English

SPMV: at least 3 credits include Mathematics and a Technical / Vocational subject and a pass in English

UEC: at least 3 Bs include Mathematics and one Science related subject

Recognized equivalent qualifications: Standard Minimum with CGPA 2.0 and above

Core Subject

Introduction to Computing, C Programming, Technical Mathematics, Calculus I, Calculus II, Engineering Mathematics, Engineer and Society, Digital Techniques I, Digital Techniques II, Microprocessors, Physics I , Physics II, Electric Circuits I, Electric Circuits II, Electronics I, Electronics II, Project & Practice I, Project & Practice II, Software application & Simulations, PLCs, Control Systems, Introduction to Electromagnetics

Course Duration

2 years 4 months full-time course (Total credits required: 94 credits)
